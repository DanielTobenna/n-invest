Renewable Energy

Nomura Finance is a fund management company that invests in a range of asset classes, including renewable energy. The company may invest in renewable energy through various means.

Direct investment

Nomura Finance invests directly in renewable energy projects such as wind farms, solar farms, or hydroelectric power plants. These investments can provide the company with a direct stake in the revenue generated by the project.

Equity investments

The company may invest in the equity of renewable energy companies that are publicly traded or privately held. This investment provides Nomura Finance with a stake in the company's earnings, and potentially benefit from any growth in the value of the company.

Debt investments

Nomura Finance also invests in the debt of renewable energy projects or companies. These investments typically provide a fixed return, and the company may be able to benefit from the growth of the renewable energy industry.

Funds

Another way Nomura Finance invest in renewable energy is through funds that are dedicated to the sector. These funds are invested in a range of renewable energy projects or companies, providing Nomura Finance with exposure to a diversified portfolio of assets. Overall, Nomura Finance's approach to investing in renewable energy depends on factors such as the company's investment strategy, risk tolerance, and investment objectives. The company may also consider factors such as the regulatory environment, technological advances, and market trends when making investment decisions in the renewable energy sector.

Risk warning: The value of investments, and the income from them, can go down as well as up and an investor may get back less than the amount invested. Past performance is not a guide to future results.
